SARS-CoV-2

The name of the virus that causes COVID-19 is SARS-CoV-2. SARS stands for "Severe Acute Respiratory Syndrome". The virus is believed to have originated from a bat, like the first SARS disease in 2003, but went through an intermediate host in a Wuhan wet market before jumping to a human. The animal origin of the virus is makes COVID-19 a zoonotic disease.

"CoV" stands for coronavirus. Coronaviruses are a family of RNA viruses that cause disease in animals and birds. "Corona" means "crown" in Latin and the term "coronavirus" was coined due the spike proteins that project out from the surface of the virus, resembling a crown (shown below). The "2" means that this is the second SARS-CoV virus identified, with the first being that of the 2003 SARS outbreak in Asia.

Viral Structure

Viruses are protein packages that surround genetic material. They require a host in order to replicate. Above, we see the structure of the SARS-CoV-2 virus. Inside the virus, we see the RNA and Nucleocapsid protein (N protein). The RNA is the genetic material and the N protein forms complexes with the RNA and plays role in enhancing the efficiency of virus transcription (production of mRNA that gets translated to form proteins) and assembly (proteins assembling around the RNA to form a protein shell called a "capsid").

Surrounding the RNA and N protein is the viral envelope, which protects the genetic material. The hemoglutinin-esterases are glycoproteins which recognizes the host receptor and acts as an enzyme to destroy the receptor. The spike glycoprotein is the primary structure that allows the virus to infect a host cell. In the initial step that leads to COVID-19, this spike protein connects to the ACE2 receptor of the host cell, which is most abundant in the lungs. Many drugs undergoing development have found potential molecules that can prevent the spike protein of SARS-CoV-2 from attaching to the ACE2 receptor of the host cell.
