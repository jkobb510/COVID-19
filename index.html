SARS-CoV-2

The name of the virus that causes the disease, COVID-19, is SARS-CoV-2. SARS stands for "Severe Acute Respiratory Syndrome".

"CoV" stands for coronavirus. Coronaviruses are a family of RNA viruses that cause disease in animals and birds. "Corona" means "crown" in Latin and the term "coronavirus" was coined due the spike proteins that project out from the surface of the virus, resembling a crown (shown below). The "2" means that this is the second SARS-CoV virus identified, with the first being that of the 2003 SARS outbreak in Asia.
